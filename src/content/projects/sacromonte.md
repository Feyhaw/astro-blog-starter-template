title: Passing on the Candle Flame
subtitle: Preservation of the 14th chapel of Sacro Monte di Varese 

description: A study of the decay situation and proposal for preservation and rennovation

abstract: | 
    Looking down to the south at the fourteenth chapel, the vast plain of Lombardy spans kilometres away until it vanishes on the horizon. The thin line that encloses our vision, yet opens up the gate for fantasies: what’s beyond that limit? The villages? Mountains? Seas? The East? Or the salvation at the end of the painful journey undertaken by the braves and the resolute.
    
    On the North, up on the hill sits the ancient village. The white glares in the background come from the Alps – another distant and seemingly unapproachable realm. Could there be anywhere closer to heaven than the top of the mountains? Could there be anything more relieving than ascending to the sky?
    
    The fourteenth chapel, the last one before the sanctuary along the two-kilometre path; standing still, guarding the belief and the devotion of generations of craftsmanship. We’ve seen events, but that’s not history. The history is the collection of stories, the memories, and what we value the most as it defines what we are. 
    
    All have come too fast and faded too soon in the eyes of earth. A million years ago, the shallow sea receded as the landscape rose into the mountains. People came and left; cities thrived and fell; civilisations grew and clashed. Sacro Monte of Varese witnessed and participated in the drastic changes of the region. It is where nature and culture resonate, as well as the mortals' messages to the divine. The chapel froze time so people of today can explore their past, as such fire shall be preserved and passed to the future. The Fourteenth chapel? Literature referred to it in so many ways, but perhaps the Bible characterised it the best: It is the jumping flame on the candle that enlightens the final approach to destiny.
    
    In the same way, let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven. (Mathew 5:16)
