title: Rails Through the Bush 
subtitle: An investigation of gauge standardization in southern Ontario

description: Research on how railway standards are made and decided in Toronto

abstract: | 
    This research shows that railway regional gauge unification, as a phenomenon of infrastructure development, can be largely explained by the framework of network externalities and path dependence. We should not forget, though, that infrastructure, as a technology, is also chosen for its technical performance. However, when debates of technological advantages remain unsettled, perhaps popular acceptance of a particular infrastructure becomes the determining factor.
    
    Looking back at this history, the development of the railway network can be seen to fit into Freud’s notion of organs: the infrastructures are auxiliary organs that extend beyond their preconditioned capabilities. We become liberated in this extended network, within which there is enhanced communication, not pointing towards any particular places, but to wherever the network exists.
