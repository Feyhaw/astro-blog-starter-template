title: A Decade in Fukushima
subtitle: Home, radiation, cursed land

description: Historical review of the Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear power plant fallout

abstract: | 
    
    Conceived in the 1950s, Fukushima Daiichi nuclear power plant remains a legacy of Cold War geopolitics and economy. It was a product of the LDP-led Japanese postwar economy and American technology. On the one hand, it stands for technological optimism and political design that nuclear power could relieve the country of the shortage of natural resources. On the other hand, it had to combat the natural disasters of the region, dysfunctional safety monitoring mechanisms, and the fear of the uncontrolled release of nuclear energy. 
    
    This documentary reviews the history of the power plant and the development of the 2011 nuclear fallout. It shows not only that the incident could have been avoided, but also how the unique site conditions contributed to the loss of control.

    Note: the narration is in Mandarin Chinese.
