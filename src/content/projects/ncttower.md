description: Historical and Architectual review of the Nagakin Capsule Tower

abstract: | 
    Sitting in the heart of Tokyo and unveiled in 1972, the Nagakin Capsule Tower stood for 50 years as a model of the 1960s sci-fi fantasies, urban utopia, and a shiny example of Metabolism. 50 years later, it reached its crossroads: Tokyo seems to have abandoned this living fossil. The debates around the building offer us a reexamination of the scope and value of Metabolism architecture. 

    Born into the chaotic yet lively city 50 years ago, we can still perceive the aura of a promising future radiating from the tower: as the name suggests, this Metabolism work of art represents a growing spirit and power. Yet after the oil crisis, neo-liberal reforms and the lost decades, the world it was born into has faded away. People seemed to have forgotten the capsule tower and the future that never happened. Metabolism seemed to have reduced to a superficial biological metaphor, in which expression overwhelmed function. But as an art icon, it is becoming something more.     

    This documentary is devoted to the 50th anniversary of the Nagakin Capsule Tower.

    Note: the narration is in Mandarin Chinese.
