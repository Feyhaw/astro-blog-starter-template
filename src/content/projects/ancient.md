abstract: | 
    These days, the city of Mianyang merely remains as a lower-tier city in China's economic and political landscape. Mianyang locates 90 kilometres North of Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan Province, and has played the vital role in the regional history. Sitting between three hills and three rivers, Mianyang's urban fabric was deeply shaped by its topography. Arguably, the modern technology of construction and transportation allow Mianyang some degrees of freedom in expanding outward from its hostroic core. Since the new regime of 1949, Mianyang saw dramatic transformation in its planning. The ancient city walls and old buildings were torn down to make way for the emerging military-industrial hub. In addition, the method of creating referenceable scaled maps was not imported to China until the twentieth century. Thus, the old city maps, if any, do not fully reflect the ancient urban landscape. It is as if the city's identity was lost and open to be rewritten. 
    
    The recovery of Mianyang's ancient city map not only tells a story of the city, how it thrived and declined around the rivers, but also shows China's history over the past 2000 years from a local perspective. Understanding Mianyang's history is key to understanding China's. Began as a fortress, Mianyang once sat on the frontier of the Han Empire. Over the dynasties, it grew into a major habitat in Sichuan. Mianyang and Chengdu sit in a basin that was naturally guarded by tall mountains on the West and North, and difficult, if not impossible, topography on the East and South. Given the land's isolation yet fertility, Sichuan easily claims its status as the most rebellious "inner kingdom" of China. The new conqueror of China, therefore, must find his way to take over Sichuan. In doing so, he must take over Chengdu, which made Mianyang the final line of defence. This project is driven solely by my personal interest and is aided by my friend, Liqi Zhang. We appreciate the Mianyang Library for its generous assistance in providing local historical records and archival materials.
